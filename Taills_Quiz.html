Quiz on Making Tails

  • 1. What does the nap of the fabric mean?
    • a. The direction that the fabric runs
    • b. The part where two pieces are sewn
    • c. The top part you leave open to stuff
    • d. Sewing is too tiring, take a nap.

    a. The direction that the fabric runs


  • 2. What type of foam should you use?
    • a. Upholstery foam
    • b. Any kind
    • c. Styrofoam
    • d. High density foam

    a. Upholstery foam


  • 3. What will stuffing lightly do?
    • a. Make the tail softer and squishier
    • b. Make the tail harder and keep its shape better
    • c. Make the tail look more realistic
    • d. Make gluing it shut easier

    a. Make the tail softer and squishier


  • 4. Why are the foam pieces added?
    • a. To make the tail keep its shape better
    • b. To make it attach easier to a belt
    • c. To keep the tail from falling apart
    • d. To make the tail stick out realistically

    d. To make the tail stick out realistically


  • 5. Should you glue the elastic onto the tail?
    • a. Yes, its stronger than sewing
    • b. No, the elastic will melt
    • c. Both, the stronger the better
    • d. Maybe, I can't really remember

    c. Both, the stronger the better


  • 6. Which side is the right side of the fabric?
    • a. The furry side, because it's the side you see
    • b. The backing side, because it's the side you work with
    • c. The edge that runs parallel to the nap
    • d. It's fabric, they're all right sides

    a. The furry side, because it's the side you see


  • 7. When cutting with a craft knife, what is the cutting mat for?
    • a. Stopping you from cutting your table or floor
    • b. Providing a backing so cutting is easier
    • c. Acting as a marker so you know how far to cut
    • d. It's not used with the knife

    a. Stopping you from cutting your table or floor


  • 8. Should you cut the fur fibres?
    • a. Yes, they're part of the fabric
    • b. No, just cut the backing
    • c. It doesn't matter, you're not going to see it anyway
    • d. Only if you're using two different colours

    b. No, just cut the backing


  • 9. A seam is:
    • a. The part where two pieces of fabric are sewn together
    • b. The direction that the fur runs
    • c. The part you aren't meant to cut off
    • d. The top part that attaches to the belt

    a. The part where two pieces of fabric are sewn together


  • 10. What foam pieces are used?
    • a. Two circles the same size
    • b. Three circles of different sizes
    • c. Three circles the same size
    • d. Two circles of different sizes

    d. Two circles of different sizes
